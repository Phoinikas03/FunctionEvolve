**Linear independence argument:** The function $\sin(\log(A+1))$ is oscillatory (it oscillates as $A \to \infty$ since $\log(A+1) \to \infty$). No combination of power-law, exponential, and polynomial functions can reproduce this oscillatory behavior. These are fundamentally different basis functions.

$\sin(\log(A+1))$ is not a linear function — it's a bounded oscillatory function that cannot be represented as $k_3 A + k_4$ for any constants.
