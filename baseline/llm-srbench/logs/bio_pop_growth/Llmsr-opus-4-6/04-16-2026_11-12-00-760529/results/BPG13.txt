The candidate implements:

$$f(t, P) = a_0 \cdot P^{a_1} \cdot (1 - (P/K)^c) + a_4 + a_5 \cdot P$$

$$f = a_0 \cdot P^{a_1} \cdot (1 - P^c / K^c) + a_4 + a_5 \cdot P$$

## Step 2: Analyze the Ground Truth

The GT is:

$$f_{GT} = c_0 \cdot \left(-1 + \frac{P}{c_2}\right)\left(1 - \frac{P}{c_4}\right) P + c_5 P + \frac{c_6 P}{1 + \exp(-c_8(-c_9 + P))}$$

Let me expand the first term:
$$c_0 \cdot \left(\frac{P}{c_2} - 1\right)\left(1 - \frac{P}{c_4}\right) P$$

This is a cubic polynomial in P:
$$c_0 \cdot P \cdot \left(\frac{P}{c_2} - 1\right)\left(1 - \frac{P}{c_4}\right) = c_0 P \left(\frac{P}{c_2} - \frac{P^2}{c_2 c_4} - 1 + \frac{P}{c_4}\right)$$

So the GT has three components:
1. A **cubic polynomial** in P
2. A **linear term** $c_5 P$
3. A **sigmoidal term** $\frac{c_6 P}{1 + \exp(-c_8(P - c_9))}$

## Step 3: Compare basis functions

The candidate has:
- $a_0 \cdot P^{a_1} \cdot (1 - P^c/K^c)$: This is a generalized power-law growth term
- $a_4$: A constant
- $a_5 \cdot P$: A linear term

**Critical observation:** The GT contains a **sigmoidal term** $\frac{c_6 P}{1 + \exp(-c_8(P - c_9))}$. This is a logistic sigmoid multiplied by P.

The candidate has NO sigmoid/exponential component. It only has power-law terms, a constant, and a linear term.

Can we set $c_6 = 0$ to eliminate the sigmoid? Let's check: $c_6 = c_0 = 0.201...$, and $c_0$ also appears as the coefficient of the cubic term. Since $c_0 = c_5 = c_6$ in the GT, setting $c_6 = 0$ would also zero out the cubic and linear terms (since they share the same constant value). But wait — the GT constants are **FIXED**. We cannot change them.

So the GT definitively contains a sigmoidal component that cannot be zero.

Can the candidate produce a sigmoid? The candidate's terms are:
- $P^{a_1}(1 - P^c/K^c)$: This is a difference of power functions. No matter what values $a_1$ and $c$ take, this remains a combination of power functions $P^{a_1} - P^{a_1+c}/K^c$.
- A constant $a_4$
- A linear term $a_5 P$

Power functions are linearly independent from sigmoid functions. There is no choice of parameters that can make a sum of power functions equal a sigmoid function over all P ≥ 0.

## Step 4: Verify the mismatch

The GT's sigmoidal term $\frac{c_6 P}{1 + \exp(-c_8(P-c_9))}$ with $c_6 \approx 0.201$ and $c_8 \approx 5.637$ creates a smooth step-like transition around $P \approx c_9 \approx 0.634$. This behavior cannot be replicated by any finite combination of power-law terms.

By the principle of **linear independence of basis functions** (Rule 3), power functions cannot exactly equal a sigmoid for all values of P.

## Conclusion

The candidate lacks the sigmoidal component present in the GT, and no parameter assignment can create one from power-law terms. The candidate cannot be made equivalent to the GT.
