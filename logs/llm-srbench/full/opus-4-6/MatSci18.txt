**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Hooke's law (linear elastic): sigma = c0 * epsilon (linear stress-strain at small deformations)
- Temperature-dependent Hooke's law: sigma = c0 * epsilon * (c1 - T) (elastic modulus decreasing linearly with temperature)

- Arrhenius thermal softening: sigma = c0 * epsilon**c1 * exp(c2 * (c3 / T)) (thermally activated deformation)
- Ludwik equation: sigma = c0 + c1 * epsilon**c2 (yield stress plus power-law hardening)
- Swift hardening: sigma = c0 * (epsilon + c1)**c2 (power-law with pre-strain offset)
- Exponential thermal softening: sigma = c0 * epsilon * exp(-c1 * T) (modulus with exponential temperature dependence)
- Polynomial stress-strain: sigma = c0 * epsilon + c1 * epsilon**2 + c2 * epsilon**3 (nonlinear elastic response)

- Combined model: sigma = (c0 + c1 * epsilon**c2) * exp(-c3 * T) (power hardening with exponential thermal softening)
