**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Zero-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 (constant rate, independent of concentration)
- First-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A (rate proportional to concentration)
- Second-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**2 (rate proportional to square of concentration)
- nth-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 (general power-law rate)
- Mixed order with offset: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 * A**2 (competing first and second order)
- Reversible first-order: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 (forward and reverse reactions)

- Fractional order with equilibrium: dA_dt = c0 * (A - c1)**c2 (approach to equilibrium)
- Cooperative kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 / (c2 + A**c1) (Hill-type kinetics)
- Parallel reactions: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 * A**c2 (multiple reaction pathways)
- Damped kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A * exp(c1 * t) + c2 (time-modulated decay with baseline)

- Simple power-law dependence on concentration → nth-order rate law dA_dt = c * A^n where n is the reaction order
- Rate approaching zero as A approaches a nonzero value → reversible reaction with equilibrium term (A - A_eq)
- Rate that changes sign → reversible or autocatalytic mechanism with competing forward/backward terms
