Classical rate laws relate reaction rates to powers of concentration, and time-dependence may arise from external forcing, photochemical effects, or implicit dependence through other species.

**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Zero-order rate law: dA_dt = c0 (constant rate, independent of concentration)
- First-order rate law: dA_dt = c0 * A (rate proportional to concentration)
- Second-order rate law: dA_dt = c0 * A**2 (bimolecular reaction kinetics)
- nth-order rate law: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 (general power-law rate expression)
- Mixed order with offset: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 * A**2 (competing first and second order)
- Reversible first-order: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 (forward and reverse reaction with constant product)
- Michaelis-Menten type: dA_dt = c0 * A / (c1 + A) (enzyme-catalyzed or saturation kinetics)
- Autocatalytic: dA_dt = c0 * A * (c1 - A) (logistic-type autocatalytic reaction)
- Time-dependent rate: dA_dt = c0 * A * exp(c1 * t) (time-varying rate coefficient)
- Photochemical decay with time: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 * t (linear time-dependent contribution)
- Inhibition kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A / (c1 + A**2) (substrate inhibition)
- Power-law with time modulation: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 * t**c2 (fractal or anomalous kinetics)
- Cooperative kinetics (Hill-type): dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 / (c2 + A**c1) (cooperative binding/reaction)

**Formula structure heuristic features**:
- Oscillation damping envelope → Exponential decay envelope f(x)*exp(-c*Abs(x)) for symmetric damped oscillation, Gaussian envelope f(x)*exp(-c*x**2) for localized wave packets

- Growth/decay → Exponential growth exp(c*t), Logistic 1/(1+exp(-c*x)), power law x**c
- Threshold/activation behavior → Max(x-c, 0), piecewise functions
- Coupling/cross effects → Product terms between variables x*y, power-law coupling x**c1 * y**c2
- Integer reaction orders (1, 2, 3) correspond to molecularity of elementary reaction steps → prefer A**n with small integer n
- Conservation of mass in closed systems means rate expressions often involve terms like A*(c-A) reflecting total concentration constraints
- Enzymatic or catalytic mechanisms produce rational functions of concentration (Michaelis-Menten form) from quasi-steady-state approximations
- If dA/dt depends explicitly on time t, this suggests an open system with external input/removal or time-varying conditions (temperature ramp, light intensity)
- Negative coefficients on concentration terms indicate consumption/decay; positive coefficients indicate production or autocatalysis
- Fractal kinetics in heterogeneous media introduce time-dependent rate 'constants' often as power laws in t
- Sequential reaction mechanisms (A→B→C) when reduced to single-variable form often yield sums of exponential terms in t multiplied by A
- Competing parallel reactions manifest as sums of power-law terms with different exponents in A
