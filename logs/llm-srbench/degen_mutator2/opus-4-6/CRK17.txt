**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Zero-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 (constant rate, independent of concentration)
- First-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A (rate proportional to concentration)
- Second-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**2 (rate proportional to concentration squared)
- nth-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 (general power-law rate law)
- Reversible first-order: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 (forward and reverse reactions with equilibrium)
- Mixed-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 * A**2 (competing first and second order pathways)

- Autocatalytic reaction: dA_dt = c0 * A * (c1 - A) (logistic-type autocatalysis)
- Inhibition kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A / (c1 + A**2) (substrate inhibition)
- Time-dependent decay: dA_dt = c0 * A * exp(c1 * t) (time-varying rate constant)
- Langmuir-Hinshelwood: dA_dt = c0 * A / (c1 + A)**2 (surface reaction kinetics)
- Fractional order with offset: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 + c2 (generalized rate with constant source/sink)
- Cooperative kinetics (Hill-type): dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 / (c2 + A**c1) (cooperative binding/reaction)

**Formula structure heuristic features**:
- Oscillation damping envelope → Exponential decay envelope f(x)*exp(-c*Abs(x)) for symmetric damped oscillation, Gaussian envelope f(x)*exp(-c*x**2) for localized wave packets

- Growth/decay → Exponential growth exp(c*t), Logistic 1/(1+exp(-c*x)), power law x**c
- Threshold/activation behavior → Max(x-c, 0), piecewise functions
- Coupling/cross effects → Product terms between variables x*y, power-law coupling x**c1 * y**c2
- Simple integer-order rate laws → dA/dt as a monomial in A: c*A^n where n=0,1,2,3 reflects molecularity of elementary steps
- Equilibrium/reversibility → additive terms with opposite signs: c0*A + c1 or c0*A - c1*A^2, indicating forward and reverse reactions
- Enzyme or surface saturation → rational functions of A: numerator linear in A, denominator with (c + A) structure, reflecting limited active sites
- Autocatalytic behavior → product of A and (c - A), reflecting both reactant consumption and product-catalyzed acceleration
