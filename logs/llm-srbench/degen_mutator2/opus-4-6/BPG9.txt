**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Exponential growth: dP_dt = c0 * P (unlimited growth, no density dependence)
- Logistic growth: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) (density-dependent growth with carrying capacity)
- Gompertz growth: dP_dt = c0 * P * log(c1 / P) (asymmetric sigmoidal growth)
- Theta-logistic: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - (c1 * P)**c2) (generalized logistic with shape parameter)
- Allee effect (strong): dP_dt = c0 * P * (P - c1) * (1 - c2 * P) (minimum viable population threshold)
- Allee effect (weak): dP_dt = c0 * P * (P + c1) * (1 - c2 * P) (reduced growth at low density without extinction threshold)
- Logistic with harvesting: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) - c2 * P (density-dependent growth with proportional removal)
- Logistic with constant harvesting: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) - c2 (growth with constant removal rate)
- Time-dependent logistic: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) * exp(c2 * t) (changing growth rate over time)
- Richards growth: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - (c1 * P)**c2) * c3 (flexible asymmetric growth curve)
- Beverton-Holt continuous: dP_dt = c0 * P / (1 + c1 * P) - c2 * P (growth with density-dependent recruitment)
- Cubic population model: dP_dt = c0 * P + c1 * P**2 + c2 * P**3 (polynomial density dependence with multiple equilibria)
- Oscillatory carrying capacity: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P * (1 + c2 * sin(c3 * t))) (seasonal environmental variation)
- Von Bertalanffy type: dP_dt = c0 * P**c1 - c2 * P (anabolic-catabolic balance)

**Formula structure heuristic features**:
- Oscillation damping envelope → Exponential decay envelope f(x)*exp(-c*Abs(x)) for symmetric damped oscillation, Gaussian envelope f(x)*exp(-c*x**2) for localized wave packets
- Saturation/clipping effects → Rational fraction x/(1+x), tanh; for variables with alternating positive/negative in oscillatory systems, selectively apply Abs
- Growth/decay → Exponential growth exp(c*t), Logistic 1/(1+exp(-c*x)), power law x**c
- Threshold/activation behavior → Max(x-c, 0), piecewise functions
- Coupling/cross effects → Product terms between variables x*y, power-law coupling x**c1 * y**c2
- Density-dependent regulation → quadratic terms like P*(1 - c*P) or P*(K-P) producing parabolic nullclines
- Allee effects (bistability) → cubic terms in P, producing two positive equilibria with an unstable intermediate one
- Carrying capacity → growth rate equals zero at a specific P value, implying factor (1 - c*P) or similar
- Depensation / inverse density dependence at low P → multiplicative factor (P - c) creating a lower critical threshold
- Seasonal or periodic forcing → trigonometric functions of time sin(c*t) or cos(c*t) modulating growth parameters
- Metabolic scaling in growth → fractional power laws P**c with c between 0 and 1 (e.g., 2/3 or 3/4)
- Immigration/emigration → additive constants independent of P in the growth rate equation
- Time-varying growth rate → explicit multiplicative or additive functions of t modifying the intrinsic rate parameter
