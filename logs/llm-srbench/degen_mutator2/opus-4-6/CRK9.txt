**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Zero-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 (constant rate, independent of concentration)
- First-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A (rate proportional to concentration)
- Second-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**2 (rate proportional to square of concentration)
- nth-order kinetics: dA_dt = c0 * A**c1 (general power-law rate dependence)
- Mixed order: dA_dt = c0 * A + c1 * A**2 (combination of first and second order)

- Pure concentration dependence (no explicit t) → standard autonomous rate law dA/dt = f(A), suggesting well-mixed isothermal conditions
- Explicit time dependence in rate → non-isothermal conditions, photochemical forcing, or catalyst deactivation/activation
- Integer power of concentration → elementary reaction step with molecularity equal to the power
