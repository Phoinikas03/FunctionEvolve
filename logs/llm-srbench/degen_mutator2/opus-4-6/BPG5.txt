**Domain**: population ecology / population dynamics
**Analysis**: This task models population growth rate dP/dt as a function of current population P and time t, which is the core problem of population dynamics. Classical models range from exponential and logistic growth to more complex forms with Allee effects, depensation, and time-varying parameters.

**Common formula structures in this domain**:
- Exponential growth: dP_dt = c0 * P (unlimited growth, Malthusian model)
- Logistic growth: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) (density-dependent growth with carrying capacity)
- Gompertz growth: dP_dt = c0 * P * log(c1 / P) (asymmetric sigmoidal growth common in tumors)
- Theta-logistic: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - (c1 * P)**c2) (generalized logistic with shape parameter)
- Allee effect (strong): dP_dt = c0 * P * (P * c1 - 1) * (1 - c2 * P) (minimum viable population threshold)
- Allee effect (weak): dP_dt = c0 * P * P * (1 - c1 * P) (growth rate increases then decreases with P)
- Richards growth: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - (c1 * P)**c2) (flexible asymmetric growth curve)
- Logistic with harvest: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) - c2 * P (logistic growth with proportional harvesting)
- Logistic with constant harvest: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P) - c2 (logistic growth with constant yield harvesting)
- Time-varying exponential: dP_dt = c0 * P * exp(c1 * t) (growth rate changing exponentially with time)
- Beverton-Holt continuous: dP_dt = c0 * P / (1 + c1 * P) (density-dependent with hyperbolic saturation)
- Power-law growth: dP_dt = c0 * P**c1 (fractional power growth dynamics)
- Logistic with time-dependent capacity: dP_dt = c0 * P * (1 - c1 * P * exp(c2 * t)) (seasonally or temporally varying carrying capacity)
- Von Bertalanffy type: dP_dt = c0 * P**c1 - c2 * P (anabolism-catabolism balance)

**Formula structure heuristic features**:
- Oscillation damping envelope → Exponential decay envelope f(x)*exp(-c*Abs(x)) for symmetric damped oscillation, Gaussian envelope f(x)*exp(-c*x**2) for localized wave packets
- Saturation/clipping effects → Rational fraction x/(1+x), tanh; for variables with alternating positive/negative in oscillatory systems, selectively apply Abs
- Growth/decay → Exponential growth exp(c*t), Logistic 1/(1+exp(-c*x)), power law x**c
- Threshold/activation behavior → Max(x-c, 0), piecewise functions
- Coupling/cross effects → Product terms between variables x*y, power-law coupling x**c1 * y**c2
- Density dependence (negative feedback) → Terms like P*(1 - c*P) or P/(1+c*P) where growth slows at high population
- Allee effect (positive density dependence at low P) → Cubic terms in P such as P*(P-c1)*(c2-P) or P^2*(1-c*P)
- Depensation / critical threshold → Multiplicative factor (P - c_threshold) creating a zero-crossing at nonzero P
- Metabolic scaling in populations → Power-law terms P^(3/4) or P^(2/3) reflecting allometric biological scaling
- Gompertz-type logarithmic regulation → P*log(c/P) structure indicating multiplicative rather than additive density regulation
- Immigration/emigration constant flux → Additive constant term independent of P representing external population input/output
- Time-modulated carrying capacity → Carrying capacity as function of t (e.g., sinusoidal or exponential) for changing environments
- Competing growth-decay balance → Sum of positive and negative power-law terms c0*P^a - c1*P^b modeling anabolism vs catabolism
